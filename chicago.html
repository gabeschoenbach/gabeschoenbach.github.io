# live performance

Theater, comedy, jazz. Below are some great venues.

  • The Annoyance. (Belmont Red Line) Good + weird improv
  • iO Theater. (North/Clybourn Red Line) Good improv (usually less weird, fwiw)
  • Neo-Futurists. (Andersonville) Go see the Infinite Wrench
  • Court Theater. (Hyde Park)
  • Jazz Showcase. (South Loop) Best jazz club in the city AND comfy couches
  • Kingston Mines. (Lincoln Park) Great live blues

Below are some particular good groups/shows.

  • My favorite independent improv comedy groups: Bridal Darty, Super Human, Swim Test, Peggy, Deep Schwa.
  • Improvised Shakespeare. Improv in couplets
  • The Infinite Wrench. 30 plays, 60 minutes

# food

Woefully incomplete list of some of my favorite restaurants around the city.

  • Asia on Argyle. (Argyle Red Line) Tons of great (mostly Vietnamese) restaurants. Especially recommend a banh mi from Ba Le and pho from Tank Noodle.
  • Green Street Smoked Meats. (West Loop) BBQ
  • Lao Peng You. (West Town) Unbelievable Chinese food.
  • Valois. (Hyde Park) The steak omelette absolutely rips – cash only!
  • Taqueria Moran. (California Blue Line) Best carnitas tacos in the city
  • Demera.(Uptown) Great Ethiopian food.
  • Fiya. (Andersonville) Great middle-eastern brunch and dinner
  • The Gundis. (Lakeview) Fantastic Kurdish food, especially good for brunch.
  • Cafecito. (Loop, South Loop, Gold Coast, Lincoln Park) Very good Cuban food.
  • North Buena Deli & Wine. (Buena Park) Good deli sandwiches for under $10!
  • m henry. (Andersonville) Great brunch.
  • Allende. (Lincoln Park) Very good tacos, open late so go post-Kingston Mines.

# activities

Even more woefully-incomplete list of fun things to do around the city…

  • Graceland Cemetery. Wrigleyville. Try to spot a coyote.
  • Brick of Chicago. Architectural walking tour. Choose your neighborhood!
  • Andersonville Wine Walk. They somehow pick the two most beautiful weekends of the year to do this.
