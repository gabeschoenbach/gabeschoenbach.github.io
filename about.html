# quick stuff

I grew up in Glen Ellen, California, in the heart of Sonoma Valley. I like to rock climb and run on the Chicago lakefront, though I miss exploring the mountains back home. I come from a family of musicians and enjoy playing the piano and the electric bass. I’m obsessed with making the dumbest possible jokes, as frequently as my friends will allow.

I’m a second-year PhD student in the Computer Science department at the University of Chicago, advised by Aloni Cohen. I’m interested in using theoretical computer science to tackle problems in civic life, with an eye towards legal and policy applications.

# research

I like thinking about questions that are easy to state and hard to solve. I’ve been interested in the intersection between theoretical computer science and legal theory. I also like thinking about combinatorial questions about graphs, in particular those about trees and partitions, all of which stem from my work in computational redistricting. I’m also getting interested in steganography and watermarking for large language models.

Before starting my PhD, I spent two years at the MGGG Redistricting Lab at Tufts University, working with Dr. Moon Duchin to connect the academic study of computational redistricting with real-world applications. Many of my past papers and projects stem from that work.
